"May the Force be with you" - Star Wars

One of the most iconic lines from the "Star Wars" franchise, this dialogue is often used as a blessing or well-wishing for someone. It has become synonymous with the series and has been referenced in pop culture countless times.

"I'll be back" - The Terminator

This famous line is from the 1984 movie "The Terminator," delivered by Arnold Schwarzenegger. It became one of the most recognizable catchphrases in film history and has been used in numerous contexts since its debut.

"Here's looking at you, kid" - Casablanca

Humphrey Bogart's iconic line from the 1942 classic "Casablanca" is a symbol of romance and farewell. It’s often quoted by fans of classic cinema and is a symbol of love and nostalgia.

"You can't handle the truth!" - A Few Good Men

Jack Nicholson delivers this memorable line in "A Few Good Men" (1992), directed by Rob Reiner. It's a powerful moment in the courtroom drama that has become ingrained in popular culture.

"Why so serious?" - The Dark Knight

This chilling line is delivered by Heath Ledger's Joker in "The Dark Knight" (2008). It has become one of the defining quotes of the film and is often associated with Ledger's groundbreaking performance.

"Life is like a box of chocolates" - Forrest Gump

Tom Hanks, playing Forrest Gump, delivers this line in the 1994 film "Forrest Gump." It's one of the most quoted lines in cinematic history, symbolizing the unpredictability of life.

"To infinity and beyond!" - Toy Story

This catchphrase is famously spoken by Buzz Lightyear in the "Toy Story" series, starting in 1995. It's a symbol of adventure, optimism, and the limitless possibilities of life.

"I see dead people" - The Sixth Sense

This line from "The Sixth Sense" (1999) is delivered by Haley Joel Osment’s character, Cole. It's a key moment in the film and became one of the most memorable lines from a psychological thriller.
